## Change 2: Cut Publishing Cadence From 5/Week to 4-6/Month

### Problem

The Consolidated Calendar schedules 5 articles per week (Monday through Friday), every week, for 25 consecutive weeks. That's 125 articles in roughly 6 months — approximately 20 articles per month.

The Larry Launch Action Plan (Task #62) explicitly states the target is "4-6 new high-intent articles per month" starting in June, scaling from the existing base. Earlier phases (Feb-May) are supposed to focus on fixing the existing 45 articles (Task #15: rewrite top 20 titles/metas, Task #42: fix articles 21-50).

This creates a 4x mismatch between what the action plan says and what the content plan schedules.

At 1,500-2,000 words per article, 5/week requires producing 7,500-10,000 words of SEO content every week — on top of the LinkedIn content cadence (3 posts/week, Tasks #16, #23, #28, #29, #32), the Reddit/Quora distribution work, the title/meta rewrites for the existing 45, and every other pre-launch task Rishi is executing solo.

### Required Change

Phase the publishing cadence to match the action plan:

- **Feb 17 - Mar 31 (Pre-Launch):** 2-3 new articles per week, focused exclusively on Tier 1 keywords. This gets 12-18 of the highest-impact articles live before launch day. Simultaneously, execute the title/meta rewrites on the existing 45 (Tasks #15 and #42).
- **April (Launch Month):** 1-2 new articles per week. Primary marketing energy goes to launch execution, LinkedIn content shift to "What Larry found" posts (Task #41), and monitoring early user behaviour.
- **May-September (Growth):** Scale to 4-6 articles per month as the action plan specifies. Publish Tier 2 operational content.
- **October+:** Add Tier 3 strategic content at 2-3 articles per month on top of ongoing Tier 2.

This means the 125 articles span approximately 12-15 months, not 6 months. That's realistic for a founder-led operation and produces higher-quality output.

## Change 4: Rewrite All 125 Titles Using the Audit Formula

### Problem

The SEO audit of the existing 45 articles established a title formula: **Benefit + Timeframe**, with a conversational tone that avoids corporate filler like "Complete Guide," "Comprehensive," or "Essential Tools."

The new 125-article plan does not follow this formula. Nine titles explicitly contain the flagged words:

1. "Automated Balance Sheet Reconciliation: **Complete** 2026 **Guide**"
2. "Sage Intacct Close Automation: **Complete** 2026 **Guide**"
3. "Migrate from Spreadsheets to Close Software: A Controller's Migration **Guide**"
4. "SOX Compliance Automation Software: **Complete** 2026 **Guide**"
5. "Audit Trail Software for Accounting: **Complete** Transaction History"
6. "Controller Technology Stack 2026: **Essential** Tools for Modern Controllers"
7. "Restructuring Accounting Teams for AI: A Controller's Org Design **Guide**"
8. "Finance Skills Every CEO Needs: A Leadership Survival **Guide**"
9. "Building a Data-Driven Finance Culture: A CFO's **Guide** to Organizational Change"

Beyond these 9, the broader pattern issue affects the majority of titles. Most follow the structure `[Feature Noun Phrase]: [Descriptor] for [Year/Audience]` — which is functionally the same rigid format that the existing 45 articles used before the audit recommended rewrites. Examples:

- "Machine Learning in Accounting: Practical Applications for 2026" (feature-descriptor)
- "AI Transaction Categorization: Automate GL Coding Accuracy" (feature-command)
- "Internal Controls Automation: Build Audit-Ready Processes in 2026" (feature-command)

Compare to the audit's rewrite formula applied to the existing 45:

- NEW: "Continuous Close on Autopilot: How AI Eliminates Month-End Scramble [2026]"

### Required Change

Rewrite all 125 titles using the Benefit + Timeframe formula. Each title should lead with the outcome the reader gets, not the technology category. Examples of what the rewrites should look like:

| Current Title | Rewritten Title |
|--------------|----------------|
| Bank Reconciliation Software: Best AI-Powered Tools for 2026 | Stop Manual Bank Recs: 7 AI Tools That Match Statements in Minutes [2026] |

## Change 5: Add a Meta Description Column and Pre-Write All 125

### Problem

The Operational (100) and Strategic (25) sheets contain 20 columns each, including columns for Reddit Short Post, Reddit Long Post, Quora Answer 1-3, and Social Status. But there is no Meta Description column.

The action plan (Task #15) identified title + meta description rewrites as "the highest-ROI marketing activity" for the existing articles. The SEO audit wrote exact replacement meta descriptions for all 45 existing articles using the **Result + Proof + Emotion** formula (e.g., "200+ finance teams cut close from 10 days to 3 with continuous close. Stop the month-end scramble — see how AI keeps your books audit-ready 24/7.").

Publishing 125 new articles without pre-written meta descriptions means one of two outcomes:
1. WordPress auto-generates metas from the first paragraph of each article (resulting in truncated, generic descriptions that hurt CTR)
2. Someone has to go back through all 125 articles after publication and write metas retroactively (exactly what you're doing now with the first 45 — doubling the work)

### Required Change

Add a "Meta Description" column to both the Operational and Strategic sheets. Pre-write all 125 meta descriptions using the Result + Proof + Emotion formula before any article is drafted. Each meta should be 150-160 characters, lead with a specific result or stat, and end with an emotional hook.

Formula: `[Specific result/stat]. [Proof point or mechanism]. [Emotional hook or CTA].`

Examples for Tier 1 articles:

| Keyword | Meta Description |
|---------|-----------------|
| FloQast alternatives 2026 | Teams switching from FloQast save 6+ hours per close with AI-native platforms. Compare 7 alternatives on price, features, and implementation speed. |
| best AI accounting software 2026 | We tested 10 AI accounting platforms on speed, accuracy, and close reduction. Here's the ranked list — with the scores that surprised us. |
| month-end close best practices 2026 | Finance teams using these 12 close practices finish in 3 days, not 10. Traditional methods meet AI-powered workflows for 2026. |
| reduce month-end close from 10 days to 3 | The step-by-step playbook 200+ controllers used to cut close from 10 days to 3. No new hires required — just smarter sequencing and AI. |

## Change 7: Address 5 Near-Cannibalisation Risks With Existing Articles

### Problem

Five new keywords share 3+ words with existing published articles, creating cannibalisation risk where Google may struggle to differentiate intent and split ranking authority between the pages.

**Pair 1: "close management software comparison 2026" (NEW) vs two existing articles**
- Existing: "ai close management software" — Post 629, https://blog.letskomplai.com/?p=629
  - Title: "AI Close Management Software: 2025 Platform Review"
- Existing: "close management software implementation time" — Post 828, https://blog.letskomplai.com/?p=828
  - Title: "Close Management Software Implementation Time: 2026 Guide"
- Risk: Three articles all targeting "close management software" as the core phrase. Google will pick one to rank and suppress the others.

**Pair 2: "QuickBooks enterprise close process" (NEW) vs existing**
- Existing: "enterprise close process for growing teams" — Post 727, https://blog.letskomplai.com/?p=727
  - Title: "Enterprise Close Process for Growing Teams: 2026 Guide"
- Risk: Both target "enterprise close process" — the new one just adds "QuickBooks" as a modifier.

**Pair 3: "manufacturing close process automation" (NEW) vs existing**
- Existing: "ai-powered close process automation platforms" — Post 789, https://blog.letskomplai.com/?p=789
  - Title: "AI-Powered Close Process Automation Platforms 2026"
- Risk: Both target "close process automation" core phrase.

**Pair 4: "Stacks AI close management review" (NEW) vs existing**
- Existing: "ai close management software" — Post 629, https://blog.letskomplai.com/?p=629
  - Title: "AI Close Management Software: 2025 Platform Review"
- Risk: Both cover AI close management; the Stacks review will compete for the same SERP.

### Required Change

For each pair:

1. **Differentiate the H1s aggressively.** The new article's H1 must not share the same core 3-word phrase as the existing article. For example, "close management software comparison 2026" should have an H1 like "Head-to-Head: Which Close Platform Actually Delivers?" rather than anything containing "close management software."

2. **Add explicit cross-links.** Each new article must link to the existing article with descriptive anchor text, and the existing article should be updated to link to the new one. This tells Google they're related but distinct.

3. **Differentiate search intent in the content.** The existing "AI close management software" article (Post 629) is an informational review. The new "close management software comparison 2026" must be a pure comparison piece with feature tables, pricing grids, and side-by-side screenshots. Different format = different intent signal.

4. **Consider consolidation for Pair 4.** The Stacks review (260/mo, Low) may not justify a standalone article when Post 629 already covers the space. Consider adding a Stacks section to Post 629 instead of creating a new page that will cannibalise it.

## Change 8: Build a Distribution Plan or Drop the Empty Columns

### Problem

Both the Operational and Strategic sheets include 8 distribution columns:

- Backlinks Added
- Reddit Short Post
- Reddit Long Post
- Reddit Threads (with links!)
- Quora Answer 1
- Quora Answer 2
- Quora Answer 3
- Quora Questions
- Social Status

All 125 rows have these columns completely empty. This is the same issue flagged in the existing 45 articles, where 43 out of 45 had no social content despite the columns existing.

The action plan explicitly calls for:
- Task #56: Reddit/Slack community engagement
- Task #16/#23/#28/#29/#32: LinkedIn content cadence (3x/week)
- The entire backlink strategy mentioned in the launch plan

Having empty distribution columns in a content plan creates a false sense of completeness — the spreadsheet looks like distribution is accounted for, but in practice nothing is planned.

### Required Change

Two options, either is acceptable:

**Option A (recommended): Build distribution templates for Tier 1 articles.** For the 20 Tier 1 articles, pre-write:
- 1 Reddit short post (2-3 sentences with a hook)
- 1 Reddit long post (3-4 paragraphs adding value beyond the article)
- 2-3 target Reddit threads where the article could be shared as a helpful response
- 1 Quora answer targeting the primary keyword
- 1 LinkedIn post extract for the 3x/week cadence
- A backlink outreach target list (3-5 sites per article)

This can be done for Tier 2 and 3 articles as they're published, but Tier 1 should have distribution baked in from day one.

**Option B: Remove the columns entirely.** If distribution planning will happen in a separate workflow or tool, remove the columns from this spreadsheet to avoid false signal. Add them back when there's an actual process to fill them.
