## Post 2: The "Spreadsheet Confessional" Audit
**Date:** Wednesday, Feb 12
**Topic:** Shadow ERP Inventory
**Insight:** *"Excel remains the crutchâ€”Even with ERPs, consolidation, projections, and analysis happen in Excel."* (Client Insight)

**Headline:**
Your ERP is $75,000/year.
Your real finance system is a Google Sheet named "Final_v3_ACTUAL_USE_THIS.xlsx"

**Body:**
I ran an experiment last month.

I asked 12 CFOs to send me screenshots of their "Most Used" Google Drive folders.

Every single one had a folder called something like:
- "Actuals"
- "Real Numbers"
- "Monthly Close Checklist"

Here's what I learned:
**Nobody trusts their ERP to do the job alone.**

They're paying for QuickBooks, Xero, or NetSuite. But the *real work* happens in spreadsheets that:
1. Fix missing vendor categorizations
2. Calculate multi-currency consolidations
3. Track budget vs. actuals because the ERP can't

I call this the **"Shadow ERP."**

And it's the most fragile part of your entire stack.

**The Spreadsheet Inventory Audit:**
Open your Google Drive right now.
Count how many spreadsheets exist **solely to patch your ERP.**

Ask yourself:
1. How many sheets are "fixing" data that should already be structured in your ERP?
2. If the person who built them quit tomorrow, could you recreate them?
3. How many formulas in those sheets have *zero documentation*?

**The Decision Framework:**
Here's when to kill the sheet:
â€¢ **If the sheet is correcting bad data:** Fix the ERP categorization rules instead.
â€¢ **If the sheet is doing calculations the ERP should do:** You need a better system.
â€¢ **If the sheet is a "one-off" that's been running for 18 months:** It's not one-off. Automate it.

**The Fix:**
The best finance teams I see are moving toward **a single source of truth.**

## Post 6: Budget Variance Action Plan
**Date:** Friday, Feb 14
**Topic:** Root Cause Variance Analysis
**Insight:** *"Manual budget versus actual tracking takes 2-3 days each month."* (Client Insight #7)

**Headline:**
Reporting variance isn't analysis.
It's just math.

**Body:**
Every month, the same dance:

The CEO asks: *"Why is Marketing 15% over budget?"*
The CFO says: *"Let me get back to you."*

3 days later, the CFO responds: *"It was the Q4 campaign and a vendor invoice we missed."*

By then, the CEO has already approved the next spend.

Here's the problem:
**Most finance teams stop at "What."**
They report the variance. But they don't diagnose the *why.*

I was on a call with a Controller last week who told me:
"We spend 2-3 days every month manually tracking budget vs. actuals. And even then, we only get the variance number. We don't get the story behind it."

**The "5 Whys" Framework for Variance:**
When you see a budget variance, don't just report the number.
Run this diagnostic:

1. **What is over/under?** (The Account)
2. **Why did it happen?** (The Vendor/Transaction)
3. **Why wasn't it caught?** (The Process Gap)
4. **Why does the gap exist?** (The Tool Limitation)
5. **Why haven't we fixed it?** (The Real Blocker)

Example:
1. Marketing is $15K over.
2. Because we ran an unplanned LinkedIn campaign.
3. Because it wasn't in the budget tracker.
4. Because the budget tracker is a static spreadsheet.
5. Because we don't have real-time spend visibility.

**The Action Plan Template:**
Stop accepting variance reports that look like this:
| Account | Budget | Actual | Variance |
|---------|--------|--------|----------|
| Marketing | 100K | 115K | +15K |

Start requiring reports that look like this:
| Account | Variance | Root Cause | Action |
|---------|----------|-----------|---------|
| Marketing | +15K | Unplanned Q4 campaign | Update approval workflow |

The first one is a spreadsheet.
The second one is a decision tool.

**The Fix:**
The best finance teams I see don't just track varianceâ€”they *action it.*
They use systems that automatically flag when spend is trending over budget and surface the root cause in seconds, not days.

That's what we're building with Komplai: variance analysis that explains itself.
