Finding 6: Featured Snippet Optimisation
Your Optimisation Guide targets 15-20% of articles winning position 0 featured snippets. Current articles lack the formatting required to win snippets. Specifically:
Missing: Direct-answer paragraphs in the first 50 words after the primary H2. Google pulls snippets from concise, definitional answers that directly respond to the search query.
Highest-Opportunity Snippet Targets
These keywords have definition or how-to intent that Google commonly rewards with snippets:
1. What is continuous close management?
2. What is anomaly detection in financial close?
3. Why does month-end close take so long?
4. What is maker-checker accounting automation?
5. What is automated prepaid expense amortisation?
6. What is multi-entity consolidation automation?
7. What is continuous close for CFOs?
8. What is close checklist automation?
Fix: For each article, add a bolded definition paragraph directly under the first H2 that answers the query in under 50 words. Use the format from your Optimisation Guide: "<strong>[Keyword]</strong> is [concise definition]. [One supporting fact with a number]."
Finding 7: Writing Style Misalignment
Your Optimisation Guide explicitly removes the rigid SEO Writing Rules (mandatory numbered H2s, forced 35% transition words, zero consecutive same-word starts) in favour of conversational tone. However, the earlier articles (roughly articles 1-20) were generated under the old rules and still read mechanically.
The conflict: The SEO Writing Rules file still mandates 35% transition word usage and zero consecutive same-word starts. The Optimisation Guide explicitly removes these constraints, calling them "too restrictive" and "robotic." These two documents contradict each other. The Optimisation Guide should be treated as the authoritative source going forward, and the old Writing Rules should be archived.
Recommendation: Do NOT rewrite all 45 articles for tone. Instead, combine the tone refresh with the title/meta rewrite pass. When you’re already in an article updating its title and meta, also refresh the opening 150 words to be conversational and snippet-optimised. Full content rewrites can happen in Phase 3 (June-September) as part of the “4-6 new high-intent articles per month” cadence.
